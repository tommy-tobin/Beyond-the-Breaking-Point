The Kintsugi Practice

The bowl broken open
becomes more itself

Kintsugi — the Japanese art of mending broken pottery with gold — is the animating spirit of this series. The crack is not hidden. It is honored. The break becomes the most luminous part of the whole.

In partnership with the National Center for Restorative Justice and the IIRP Graduate School, this series creates spaces where survivors, practitioners, advocates, educators, and communities gather to transform rupture into repair — and repair into systemic change.

Shasta Groene Alvarez's public courage reminds us that healing is not simple, and that justice has to reach beyond individual recovery into the systems that failed to protect, listen, and respond.

Systemic Advocacy After Rupture

When Healing Demands Justice

Personal restoration is never enough when systems failed. This series holds space for legal action, specialized protection, policy change, and the long arc toward a permanent institution rooted in restorative values.

Specialized Support & Protections

For Those Who Need More Than a Circle

Certain forms of harm require specialized safety, legal, and advocacy support that goes beyond the restorative circle itself. These pathways exist for those navigating complex systems — and for those who walk alongside them.

Future Vision · Toward a Permanent Center

From Circle to Institution: A Restorative Justice Center

The long arc of this work points toward the creation of a dedicated nonprofit — survivor-led, practitioner-guided, legally sophisticated — offering restorative processes, systemic advocacy, trauma-informed legal accompaniment, and community healing for those failed by conventional systems. This is not a destination. It is a direction. Every registration, every circle, every co-created story moves us closer. Those who enter this circle today are founding something not yet named. There is no Shasta Groene Foundation. We hold her name with humility and care, as witnesses, not as brand-builders. What we build will be named by those who build it together.

Restorative justice asks us to turn toward harm with care, truth, and accountability. The repair matters because the break mattered.

This dedication is offered with respect for Shasta Groene Alvarez's survival, public truth-telling, and life beyond what happened to her. We do not speak for her or use her name as an institution. We let her story sharpen our responsibility to build safer, more accountable systems.
